Agridera Fresh Market Lemon Hybrids

Fortunately, lemons are a recent "discovery" of botanists. Therefore, you can literally “with documents in hand” trace the emergence and development of almost every variety and illustrate the story with fruits (since they are on sale). In summer, when the main plantings of lemon trees are just beginning to form the ovary, Argentinean is sold in our stores Eureka is a modern frankly sour and not fragrant variety that appeared after the fruits of the Siciliano lemon were brought to the USA and, as it usually happens, someone curious sowed the seeds In particular, we breed improved disease-resistant cultivars adapted to different markets globally.

They are also on sale with us. These are dark yellow fruits with large dark greasy spots on the skin, which makes them look like broken apples. Siciliano is the most essential oil variety of lemons, for which it is valued.

Another well-known variety is the ever-flowering remontant Lunario lemon. It is very often sold in garden centers.

Vrika, Siciliano and Lunario belong to the Italian variety of Feminello lemons, which combines more than 20 varieties, including both industrial and collection ones.

Lemon so-called "Americans"

The so-called "Americans" are also found on the shelves of our supermarkets - lemons of Lisbon and Genoa varieties.. These plants are named after the port from which they were most often exported. And the intra-varietal diversity of this variety type appeared already on the American continent as a result of seed propagation.

Another group of lemons

Another group of lemons was bred in the Novo-Afonsky nursery, where a variety of Italian varieties of lemons were planted at different times. From those trees that did not freeze, Novogruzinsky, Kuzner, Chakvatadze, Gizenko, Udarnik and others lemons were obtained by cross-pollination. They are grown in almost every botanical garden, so cuttings and seedlings of these plants are quite affordable.

The last group of lemons

The last group of the above are Meyer lemons. They are often sold on New Year's Eve. Usually these are small orange fruits from Azerbaijan or Uzbekistan, or large, but the same orange-orange lemons from Turkey with a coniferous flavor of zest. These plants have nothing to do with lemons, but they grow remarkably and bloom in houses, tolerate frosts and, when propagated by seed, bloom in the 5-7th year after sowing.
